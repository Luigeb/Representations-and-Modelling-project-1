Before you can start to build your very own PC, you need to buy all the components needed, and of course some accesories if you want it to look nice and pretty. The main 6 Components you need to build your very own PC are:

  2. Motherboards
  3. Central Processing Unit (CPU)
  4. Memory (RAM)
  5. Storage
  6. Power Supply (PSU)
  7. Graphic Processing Unit (GPU)

Central Processing Unit (CPU)

The CPU is often referred to as the central or main processor. It runs the machines apps and operating systems and is made up of a series of intricate wires and circuits. You can think of the CPU as basically the brain of the computer and it tell the other components what to do and when to do it. When a PC is used for specifically gaming the CPU is very important as it is used for game logic and AI calculations, asset streaming and loading, input processing, and physics simulations. The most popular developers for CPUs are "AMD RYZEN" and "Intel Core". CPUs can be quite pricey but they are a key component for frame rates and gameplay settings.

Power Supply (PSU)

Buy your components over time to get the best deal. In-store locations such as Best Buy will stock computer components, but you can usually find comparable parts for cheaper if you shop online. Common online locations include Amazon, eBay, and NewEgg. If you want the best prices, wait for sales on the individual component in your list—you’d be surprised how often parts go on sale. Waiting for sales (e.g. Black Friday Sales) can often pay off, as parts can be heavily discounted, allowing you to either save money or opt for better components for the same price. Some online retailers may offer a 'free shipping' deal if you spend a certain amount of money. Consider buying enough parts from those sites to save on shipping, which is often expensive. This doesn't mean you should pay more for components cheaper elsewhere though!
