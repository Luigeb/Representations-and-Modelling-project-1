Before you can start to build your very own PC, you need to buy all the components needed, and of course some accessories if you want it to look nice and pretty. The main 6 Components you need to build your very own PC are:

  1. Case
  2. Motherboards
  3. Central Processing Unit (CPU)
  4. Memory (RAM)
  5. Storage
  6. Power Supply (PSU)
  7. Graphic Processing Unit (GPU)

A motherboard connects all the components together. It allows the CPU, GPU, hard drive and more to talk to one another in one spot.

Central Processing Unit (CPU)

The CPU is often referred to as the central or main processor. It runs the machines apps and operating systems and is made up of a series of intricate wires and circuits. You can think of the CPU as basically the brain of the computer and it tell the other components what to do and when to do it. When a PC is used for specifically gaming the CPU is very important as it is used for game logic and AI calculations, asset streaming and loading, input processing, and physics simulations. The most popular developers for CPUs are "AMD RYZEN" and "Intel Core". CPUs can be quite pricey but they are a key component for frame rates and gameplay settings.

Memory keeps where you last were on each of these tasks so you dont loose progress of what you are preforming. Most everyday devices have about 8 GB of RAM, for video editing, or streaming videos. Storage (SSD)

Storage or SSD as you might have heard it being called is like RAM, it keeps the data, files, programs, etc, going even when your device is powered off or not in use. Storage can always be added to a computer unlike a phone, which is handy if you are planning on not ever switching from your beutiful PC that you are building. it is always handy to have a good bit of storage in your PC, with the reccomended storage being about 512 GB or 1,024 GB. You may be wondering the difference between HHD (Hybrid Hard Drive) and SSD (Solid-State Drive). Hybrid Hard Drives are heavier and slower hard drives. They do the same function but SSDs are faster and don't make as much noise or produce as much heat.
